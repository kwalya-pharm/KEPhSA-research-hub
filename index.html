The KEPhSA Research Hub

     

An affiliate of the Kenya Pharmaceutical Student's Organization (KEPhSA)

           
       
         

VISION

         

Empower and inspire the next generation of pharmacy students by establishing a dynamic research hub.

       
               
         

MISSION

         

Provide pharmacy students with a platform to explore, experiment, and excel, ultimately shaping the future of healthcare through their research contributions.

       
     
     
       

OBJECTIVES

       
             
  • Provide pharmacy students with access to research facilities and equipment.
    •          
  • Offer mentorship and guidance from experienced researchers.
    •          
  • Promote collaboration and networking among pharmacy students and researchers.
    •          
  • Contribute to the advancement of the pharmacy profession in Kenya.
    •        
     
   
   
     

Membership

           
       

Requirements for Membership

       

All pharmacy students from local chapters are eligible for membership.

       
             
  • Pay a non-refundable registration fee of Ksh 200
    •          
  • Subscribe to KEPhSA Research Hub annually
    •        
     
     
       

Members’ Rights and Responsibilities

       

Members have a range of rights and responsibilities to help them engage with the Hub.

       
             
  1. Attend any Hub meeting
    2.          
  2. Receive communications from the Executive Committee
    3.          
  3. Access assistance as per the Constitution
    4.          
  4. Participate in Hub discussions and decisions
    5.          
  5. Publish research articles
    6.          
  6. Attend specialized research training
    7.          
  7.            

    Full members also have the right to:

               
                   
    • Vote on motions in General or Special Meetings
      •              
    • Nominate candidates for committees
      •              
    • Be eligible for nomination or appointment to office
      •            
             
    8.        
     
     
       

Termination of Membership

       

Membership can be terminated under specific conditions as outlined below.

       
             
  1.            

    Automatic termination occurs:

               
                   
    • Upon deregistration from the School of Pharmacy
      •              
    • Upon acceptance of resignation by the Executive Committee
      •              
    • Upon removal by Council in accordance with the Constitution
      •            
             
    2.          
  2. Subscriptions are non-refundable upon termination.
    3.          
  3. Members may resign in writing, with terms determined by the Executive.
    4.          
  4. Expulsion follows disciplinary proceedings.
    5.          
  5. Re-admission is subject to Executive and Council discretion.
    6.          
  6. Expelled members may be reinstated at the Executive’s discretion.
    7.        
     
     
       

Discipline and Disciplinary Action

       

All complaints must be submitted in writing to the Executive Committee for appropriate action per the Constitution.

     
   
   
     

Leadership

     

All members are eligible to be nominated for the Executive Committee.
